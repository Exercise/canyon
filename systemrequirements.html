System requirements

You must have a webcam, speakers and microphone to conduct video coaching sessions. And, like any software, our video chat technology can only run (and run its best) on computers that meet certain system requirements. Please review these in advance of signing up to make sure your system is compatible. Tablet compatibility coming soon!

Your computer

Processor

Minimum:
Intel® Core™ i3 or equivalent AMD processor

Recommended:
Intel® Core™ i5 or equivalent with an automatic burst of speed when needed from Intel® Turbo Boost Technology 2.0; 3rd Gen Intel® Core™ i73770 (Quad Core, 3.40GHz, 8MB w/HD4000 Graphics)

Single-core machine: CPU speed greater than 3.0 GHz

Dual-core or tri-core machine: CPU speed greater than 2.5 GHz

Quad-core machine: CPU speed greater than 1GHz

For higher video resolutions and higher frame rates, a more powerful processor is recommended.

An Atom or Celeron processor is not recommended.

Webcam

You will need a high-quality webcam; we recommend the Logitech Pro range of webcams. We encourage you to sit in well-lit space during video sessions, as insufficient lighting may result in blacked-out or low video quality.

Microphone & Speakers

A good microphone yields a high-fidelity audio experience. Apple iMac and Macbook microphones, as well as most mobile microphones, have built-in echo cancellation abilities. When using a headset, make sure the mic is close to your mouth and that the headset mic is selected from the settings within the live video screen (click the gear icon in the live session window to adjust).

your internet connection

Network Requirements:
Minimum: 2Mbps download 2Mbps upload speed
Recommended: 8 Mbps upload speed and 6 Mbps download speed or higher
(You can test your connection at www.speedtest.net.)

A round trip time (ping) of less than 300 milliseconds is required for a natural experience.

Important Information About Use
in Commercial Environments

Video and Audio Sessions
Video is provided over random local ports that include both TCP and UDP. In general, to use audio and video functionality, your computer needs to be able to communicate with following remote ports:

  • UDP 540 – audio streaming
  • UDP 541 – video streaming
  • TCP 443 – A/V streaming, HTTPS fallback communication, used when default
    UDP streaming fails

Proxies
The HealthFleet plugin (which you are prompted to install when you log on to your dashboard) uses the Web Proxy information configured in a browser to access its remote services.
